Here you'll find examples of the learning materials used throughout my training, and some projects I've done during my 4 months with Per Scholas. GLABS or Guided Labs, explained and showed examples of specific processes from beginning to end and my responsibility was to practice it on my own and explore what happens when things go wrong, when they go right, and understand the "why" behind it. Prior to each module, I studied the material from slide decks to prepare with the foundational knowledge before actively practicing. Additionally, I made note of gaps in my knowledge at every step and researched to find the answers to questions that came up. 

This program began November 10, 2025, and concluded on March 6, 2026. I attended class every single day Monday – Friday from 9am – 4pm throughout its entirety and put in countless extra hours on weeknights, weekends, and holidays. During this time, I worked on revolving teams and engaged in daily stand-ups each morning to hold one another accountable on completing coursework and to help clear any blockers we might have come up against, if we couldn’t, we brought it to the instructor during the debrief. All the learners in this program came from varied technical experiences and backgrounds, which meant clear communication was key between us and with the instructors. This environment simulated the structure of a workplace, and it challenged all of us to expand on our soft skills while learning the technical skills.

#### As I built this repository, I was able to see a clear progression of my knowledge and quality of work from beginning to end.

I started out as a complete tech novice and through hard work, dedication, time management, and audacity, have completed an intensive data analytics bootcamp designed to build job-ready skills across the full data pipeline. The program covered foundational to advanced topics including statistical analysis, data visualization, and business intelligence reporting. Core technical training included advanced Microsoft Excel (functions, PivotTables, dashboards, and data modeling), SQL (database design, querying, joins, subqueries, and data integrity), and Power BI (DAX, data modeling, and interactive reporting). Additional coursework covered ETL processes and data transformation using PowerQuery, version control with Git, and the application of AI tools within analytics workflows. The program concluded with a Capstone Project demonstrating the ability to apply all learned skills to a real-world data problem.

#### Key Skills Gained: Excel, Statistical Analysis, SQL, Power BI, PowerQuery, ETL, Data Modeling, and Data Visualization
